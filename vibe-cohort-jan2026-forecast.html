Key Insights

✅ Margin stabilises at 86–88% from LM2 onward

After January's AI spike is absorbed, the cohort's economics are very stable. By December 2026, margin is expected at 88.8% because AI costs continue to decline while monthly subscribers pay a consistent renewal rate.

📊 Annual plan is the backbone — 77.8% of base by Jan 2027

Annual plan subscribers grow from 51.2% of the premium base (Jan 2026) to 78.6% by Dec 2026, then dip slightly to 77.8% in Jan 2027 (15% renewal churn removes 171 annual sites). The monthly base shrinks faster mid-year (11%/mo churn), but the renewal event disproportionately hits annual subscribers in month 13.

💰 Cumulative revenue by Jan 2027: ~$682K from 86,452 sites ($7.89/site)

$269K collected in Jan 2026 (annual upfront) + $245K from monthly renewals (Feb–Dec) + ~$168K from Jan 2027 annual renewal (15% churn assumed) = $682K total. Stopping at Dec 2026 gives $514K — the renewal adds +$168K more. Margin hits 99%+ in Jan 2027 as AI costs are negligible by LM13.

🔍 Watch: Jan 2027 actual renewal rate (data lands Aug 2026)

The Jul 2025 cohort annual plans (~293 sites) renew in Aug 2026 — the first real Vibe renewal churn data point. The model uses 15% (SaaS benchmark). If it's lower (5–10%), Jan 2027 revenue rises to $175K–$186K; if higher (25–30%), it drops to $133K–$145K. A ±10% swing in renewal churn = ±~$19K in Jan 2027 revenue.
