Key findings

Insights & Risks

All active premium segments remain profitable. Every active premium site covers its AI cost regardless of cost pattern. The worst case is steady + active_monthly: $11.69 AI cost vs $20.38 revenue → +$8.69 margin (1.74× TROI). The best is no_ai_cost + active_annual: pure $21.76/mo revenue. This hasn't changed — the premium TROI matrix is solid.
Renewal waves are 56.7% of 12-month revenue ($1.30M of $2.30M). Revenue is NOT in simple decline — it stays relatively flat Jul–Jan ($212–$237K) because renewal waves offset GPV base erosion. The model now correctly captures lump-sum $166 payments at each cohort's 12-month anniversary, with 15% renewal churn applied. Renewal wave size grows Jul→Jan as larger cohorts hit their anniversary, peaking with the Nov–Jan 2025/26 cohorts (~2,490–2,776 converts each).
Revenue cliff in Apr–Jun 2027: drops to $133K → $76K. The post-traffic-cliff cohorts from April–June 2026 had tiny conversion volumes (1,267 → 332 annual converts). When these cohorts renew one year later, they generate minimal renewal revenue. Apr 2027: 419 renewing sites × $166 = $70K. Jun 2027: only 110 renewing sites × $166 = $18K. This is the delayed impact of the May–Jun 2026 traffic cliff becoming visible 12 months later.
Premium AI cost is STABLE/DECLINING: $22K (Jul 2026) → $14K (Jun 2027), -36% over 12 months. W9 disproved the "growing pattern compounds" assumption. Of 3,306 January 2026 premium sites: 50% are "spike_only" (high LM1, then ~$0), 28% are "early_only" (gone after LM2), 14% are declining, and only 4.7% grow — and even those peak at LM4 ($6.87/site) and decline to $5.18 by LM6. The previously projected $57K/month by Jun 2027 was wrong.
12-month net margin: ~$1.87M on $2.30M revenue (81.4% avg) — profitable throughout. Jul 2026: 76.3% margin. Peaks at 86.0% in Jan 2027 (largest renewal month). Drops to 65.6% in Jun 2027 (tiny renewal cohort). The renewal wave model reveals a more accurate — and more volatile — cash flow picture than the old amortized model. Key risk: the Apr–Jun 2027 revenue cliff is real and structural.
Product strategy

Recommendations by Segment

Action Segment Rationale
KEEP & EXPAND active_annual (all cost patterns) Best TROI. Even growing-cost annual sites earn $15/mo margin. Prioritize annual plan upsell.
KEEP active_monthly (all patterns except steady) Profitable. Convert monthly → annual improves margin by ~$1.38/site/mo and reduces churn (6% vs 18%/mo).
OPTIMIZE COST first_month_only + free_never_converted 506K sites, -$0.10/site/mo each = -$51.6K aggregate. Cap AI usage after month 1 with no conversion signal.
CONVERT OR LIMIT early_only + free (11K sites) Low per-site loss (-$0.42) but $4.8K aggregate. Target for conversion campaigns before they churn fully.
DEPRIORITIZE churned_premium (all patterns) 8K sites, $0 revenue. No AI feature investment. Consider win-back campaign for declining/growing patterns first.
MONITOR growing + active (any plan) Profitable ($15/mo annual, $11.54/mo monthly). W9 confirms only 4.7% of premium sites show this pattern, and their cost peaks at LM4 then declines. No longer a compounding risk — but worth monitoring for behavioral shifts.
